What's actually integer (W4A8)
mac_blocks_wv (used for both X1 and X2 phases):

Inner hot loop: INT8 × INT4 × UINT6 → INT32 — pure integer MACs
8 partial int32_t accumulators per block — no FP adder latency constraints
FP32 only appears once per block: d[b] * (x_scale * (float)sw) —
a single multiply to apply the fp16 per-block scale

Why FP32 can't be avoided there
Q4_K stores two fp16 values per 256-element block: d (scale) and dmin
(minimum-subtraction scale). The final result for each block is:
  result = d × (x_scale × Σ(xi × qi × sci)) − dmin × (x_scale × Σ(xi × mni))
The d and dmin are fp16 with arbitrary magnitude — you can't represent them
faithfully in a fixed integer range. So one FP32 multiply per block (not per
element) is unavoidable.

Why you see fp16 → fp32 conversions
Q4_K/Q6_K blocks store their per-block scales (d, dmin) in fp16. The kernel
reads those fp16 headers and widens them to fp32 before applying them to the
integer MAC sums. That's the "passage" between fp16 and fp32 in swiglu.cpp:
the per-block metadata is fp16, but all arithmetic and the final output must
be fp32 to match the CPU reference.

Why activations stay FP32 throughout
Neural networks are trained in FP32/BF16, then the weights are quantized for
storage. The activations (the tensors flowing between layers) remain FP32
throughout. You can confirm this in two ways:

In lfm2.cpp:
  cur = ggml_add(ctx0, cur, ffn_out);   // residual add expects F32
And in the ggml.c builder:
  result = ggml_new_tensor(ctx, GGML_TYPE_F32, 4, ne);

The next layer's build_norm (RMSnorm) and the residual add both consume FP32.
The rule in llama.cpp is: weight tensors are quantized, activation tensors
are always FP32. The GGUF dump confirms this — every F32 entry is a norm
weight (small, cheap), every Q4_K/Q6_K is a projection weight.

Bottom line
Floats are used inside swiglu.cpp for accuracy and to match what the CPU ggml
path does — even though integers are cheaper to compute. Only the weights
(W_gate, W_up, W_down) are stored quantized; activations (x, gate values,
output) stay FP32 end-to-end because the rest of the pipeline expects FP32.
Attempting an all-integer activation path would require a different
quantization/activation scheme and revalidation of the entire inference stack.
